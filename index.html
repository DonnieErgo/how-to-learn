The main problems in learning

Neither school nor university teaches us how to study the material properly. We prepare for exams and learn the tickets. We practice solving monotonous problems in order to pass the test better, but this often ends up giving us no real knowledge. Cramming quickly wears off and is not useful.

Conclusion: you have to be able to learn as well, but for some reason not many people teach it. What should be done about it?

Specific techniques and exercises help to change the approach to learning, making it effective and engaging. These same techniques are applied to the training in the Practicum.

Learning techniques

Five practices from Barbara Oakley

  • Two types of attention

    Deep knowledge emerges when you alternate between focused and diffuse thinking.

  • Recall

    Recall what you have learned - this will allow you to connect the scattered portions of your memory.

  • Interleaving

    Learn several skills at the same time, they enrich each other.

  • Questions

    As you listen to the teacher, think of a good question that you really care about.

  • The illusion of competence

    Repeat it to yourself, write it down, tell a friend: you only think you have mastered the new topic.

Barbara Oakley's story

Ever since she was a child, Barbara was not good at maths. She considered herself a complete humanitarian, and not the cleverest one at that. In the army she studied Russian in order to get a raise, and so successfully that she was promoted to commanding officer. But in order to be promoted, she had to pass maths. That's when Barbara came up with her own approach to the exact sciences. It turned out that if you are "bad at something", your hard-won knowledge is much deeper than that of those who are clear at a glance.
